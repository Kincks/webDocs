Rabies Virus

Full name:

Rabies lyssavirus

Type:

Lyssavirus

Vectors:

Nearly any mammal, but mostly passed to humans through dogs, cats, racoons, or bats

Spreads via:

Being bitten or scratched by an infected creature, eating infected flesh

Human Fatality:

59,000 deaths worldwide per year, mostly in Africa and Asia

Prevention:

Avoiding wild and strangely behaving animals, vaccination if exposed

Rabies is human history's oldest recorded infectious disease, having been known about since at least 1500 BC. Although it can lay dormant in the body for months or even years, it will become fatal if left untreated. The disease caused by the Rabies virus is well known for its distinctive symptoms: aggression, psychosis, increased salivation, and hydrophobia (a fear of water). It can also manifest with lethergy and paralysis as its main symptoms instead, but is no less deadly.

Rabies attacks the nervous system.
