Ebola

Full name:

Zaire ebolavirus

Type:

Ebolavirus

Vectors:

Bats, humans, primates

Spreads via:

Contact with bodily fluids containing the virus, eating infected flesh

Human Mortality:

Between 25-90%, 50% die on average

Prevention:

Only medical experts in complete coverage should interact with infected people, proper cooking and handling required for bushmeat

Ebolavirus causes the Ebola Virus Disease (EVD) in those infected. This incredibly deadly disease causes a hemorrhagic fever (it's likely to cause internal and external bleeding), diarrhea, and vomiting among other common symptoms. This occurs as the virus attacks and destroys just about every cell it comes in contact with, including immune cells. Most people who die of Ebola are actually killed by the rapid fluid loss the disease causes.

EVD was discovered in 1976 near the Ebola River in the Democratic Republic of the Congo and has caused 21 outbreaks, the most recent being last year. It seems to have originated in bats who suffer no ill effects from harboring the virus but can pass the disease to people though their bushmeat. There are five strains of the Ebolavirus with Zaire ebolavirus being the most common and deadly.
