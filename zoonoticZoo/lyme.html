Lyme Disease

Full name:

Lyme borreliosis

Type:

Borrelia bacterium

Vectors:

Ticks

Spreads via:

Tick bites

Human Infections:

360,000 per year, estimated

Prevention:

Wearing insect spray, long sleeves and pants, avoiding tall grasses and brush, checking oneself and pets while removing any ticks after going outside

Lyme disease got it's name from the towns of Lyme and Old Lyme, Connecticut where there was a large number of children suffering from arthritis in 1975. Although the Borrelia bacteria have existed for a long time, the seemingly disconnected cause and symptoms made the disease difficult to identify. Most times an infected tick bite will lead to an indicative bull's-eye rash, but otherwise it can be difficult to tell when a person has become infected until they exhibit Lyme disease symptoms.

Because Lyme disease is caused by bacteria it can be treated with antibiotics. However, the Borrelia bacteria are spirochetes, a corkscrew-shaped bacteria with the ability to lie dormant in the body for long periods of time. The bacteria will congregate in the joints, heart, and nervous system, damaging them over time, permanently if left untreated. Although rarely deadly, an infected person can continue to have their symptoms flare up for months or years after treatment, even when the disease is no longer detectable in the person's body. This has led many people to believe that Lyme disease is a chronic condition.
